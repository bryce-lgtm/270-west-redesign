If you'd like to move forward, we take time to understand your full story. That means your service history, the conditions you're dealing with, any prior VAC decisions, and how your health has changed over time. We're not rushing to file — we're making sure we understand what happened so the application reflects your real situation.

We gather the evidence

A strong VAC claim is built on documentation. We help you request and organize the records VAC needs — medical records, service documents, specialist reports, and supporting statements. We know what VAC looks for and how to present evidence clearly. Most applicants are missing key pieces without realizing it; we make sure yours is complete.

Typical evidence includes
·CF 98 / service injury records
·Medical diagnosis and treatment history
·Specialist or physician reports
·Supporting statements (if needed)
·Nexus letters linking condition to service

Once we file, we keep track of your claim's progress. If VAC needs more information, we respond. Common questions

Questions about the process

Is the initial consultation really free?

Yes. The eligibility check and your first call with an advisor are completely free, with no obligation. If you want help taking it further, we'll explain exactly how our fees work before anything moves forward.

How long does a VAC application take?

The eligibility check takes about two minutes. Building and filing a complete application typically takes four to eight weeks depending on how readily available your documents are. VAC's review process can take several months — we'll set realistic expectations together on our first call.

What if my claim was already denied?

Denials are common and often reversible. A fresh look at the documentation — or a stronger nexus between your condition and your service — can make a real difference. We've helped veterans turn around denied claims at reconsideration and at VRAB. Bring us your file.

Do I need to have a diagnosis before I contact you?

No. You can reach out at any stage — even if you're just starting to think about this. Part of our job is helping you understand what documentation you'll eventually need and how to get it.

Can family members get help too?

Yes. Surviving spouses and some eligible family members may qualify for VAC benefits. Tell us your situation on the eligibility check and we'll walk you through what may apply.

Is 270 West part of Veterans Affairs Canada?

No. We're an independent advisory service. We work alongside VAC to help you put together the strongest possible application — but we're not affiliated with the government and you can always contact VAC directly.
