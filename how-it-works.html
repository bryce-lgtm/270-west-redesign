How it works

How VAC disability benefits work — and how 270 West helps Canadian veterans apply.

Three calm steps. At your pace.

From a quick eligibility check to a complete Veterans Affairs Canada application — we work alongside you, in plain language, with no pressure.

Step 1: Check your eligibility for VAC benefits

Answer five short questions about your service and what you're looking to do. We use this only to understand which Veterans Affairs Canada programs may apply — not to pre-qualify or pressure you. It's free, confidential, and you can stop anytime.

Step 2: Talk through your situation with an advisor

We schedule a no-cost call with one of our claims advisors. We listen first, walk through your situation in plain language, and explain how VAC's programs work. You decide if and when to take a next step — there's never any pressure.

What to expect
·No-cost initial call
·We listen before we advise
·You decide next steps

Step 3: Build a complete VAC application — together

If you'd like our help, we walk you through gathering documentation, understanding what VAC needs, and putting together a clear, complete application. We stay with you through any follow-up VAC may need.

What to expect

A note on our role: 270 West Consulting is an independent claims advisory service. We work alongside Veterans Affairs Canada, not in place of them — and you can contact VAC directly at any time at 1-866-522-2122 or vac.gc.ca.
Common questions

Frequently asked questions about VAC claims support

Is 270 West's eligibility check free?

The eligibility check-in and your first conversation are completely free, with no obligation. If you'd like our help with a full application, we'll explain how that works upfront — no surprises.

Is 270 West part of Veterans Affairs Canada?

No. We're an independent advisory service based in Canada. We work alongside Veterans Affairs Canada to help you put together your strongest application — but you can always contact VAC directly.

Can I apply for VAC benefits on my own?

Absolutely. VAC accepts applications directly from veterans, and many people apply on their own. We're here if you'd like a teammate to help you through it.

How long does the VAC application process take?

The eligibility check is about two minutes. From there, timelines depend on your situation and on VAC's review process. We'll set realistic expectations together on our first call.

What if my VAC claim has already been denied?

We can still help. Sometimes a fresh look at the documentation or framing of a claim makes a meaningful difference. Bring us your story and we'll walk through it together.
